Common Waste Materials

Common waste materials that can be creatively transformed through upcycling using the CPS approach.

Plastic Waste

Plastic waste is widely produced in daily activities and often ends up polluting the environment. With creative upcycling, plastic can be transformed into functional and decorative products.

Examples: Plastic bottles, plastic bags, food containers, straws, packaging materials.

Paper Waste

Paper waste is easy to recycle and suitable for creative reuse. Upcycling paper helps reduce waste while producing useful and artistic items.

Examples: Newspapers, cardboard boxes, magazines, office paper, paper bags.

Fabric Waste

Fabric waste usually comes from old or unused clothing. Through upcycling, textiles can be transformed into bags, accessories, and household items.

Examples: Old clothes, jeans, curtains, fabric scraps.

Metal Waste

Metal waste is durable and suitable for long-term reuse. With proper design, metal items can be turned into functional or decorative products.

Examples: Aluminium cans, tin cans, metal lids, wires.

Glass Waste

Glass waste is highly recyclable and durable, making it ideal for creative upcycling. With proper design, glass items can be transformed into decorative and functional household products.

Examples: Glass bottles, jars, broken glass containers, perfume bottles.
